Let me write this: Forrest Gump is ain't no idiot, and all those who think otherwise need to get their heads examined.
I picked up this book because of the hype around the movie Forrest Gump, I haven't seen the movie, but I can clearly see why people loved Gump. I know the book and the movie are quite different. And I assume that's the reason why some people didn't like the book at all. Maybe I might change my opinion once I do watch the movie. But today I'm only wondering how sometimes bein a idiot can take you to places where no sane person can ever go.
📔
LET ME SAY THIS: bein a idiot is no box of chocolates. People laugh, lose patience, treat you shabby. Now they says folks sposed to be kind to the afflicted, but let me tell you—it ain't always that way. Even so, I got no complaints, cause I reckon I done live a pretty interestin life, so to speak.
In between there is a life filled with a series of adventures (or misadventures) that lead Gump to become a star football player, a war hero, an astronaut, a cotton farmer, a national level ping-pong player, a wrestler, a national level chess player, a music band member, an entrepreneur, and few other careers that I must have forgotten. Gump can give you a FOMO about living an ordinary life. Did I tell you that he even fought the presidential elections? And yet, he's only an idiot. Is he? You decide.
📔
But let me tell you this: sometimes at night, when I look up at the stars, an see the whole sky jus laid out there, don't you think I ain't rememberin it all. I still got dreams like anybody else, an ever so often, I am thinkin about how things might of been. An then, all of a sudden, I'm forty, fifty, sixty years ole, you know? Well, so what? I may be a idiot, but most of the time, anyway, I tried to do the right thing-- an dreams is jus dreams, ain't they? So whatever else has happened, I am figgerin this: I can always look back an say, at least I ain't led no hum-drum life. You know what I mean?
It's funny on the face but a dark book by the heart. I really liked how the story came full circle in the end.
