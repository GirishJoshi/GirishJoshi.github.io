Review
This is the story of Count Alexander Ilyich Rostov, a Russian Aristocrat in the times of Revolution. He's under house arrest in a grand hotel of Moscow, Metropol. Count restarts his life because — if a Gentleman does not master his circumstances then he is bound to be mastered by them. The people he meets during his stay at Metropol help him usher his restlessness towards purposefulness. He makes friends with the staff of the hotel and builds a routine while exploring the extents of Metropol. He is sometimes visited by old friends and sometimes he finds new friends in a small world. While we learn from Count what it means to be a Gentleman, for a man who has nowhere to go often goes on the thought train of philosophical musings and self-reflection. Count teaches us that every life deserves not only our consideration but our reconsideration‚ — and our unwavering determination to withhold our opinion until we have engaged with them in every possible setting at every possible hour. He teaches us that what matters in life is not whether we receive a round of applause; what matters is whether we have the courage to venture forth despite the uncertainty of acclaim.
Count's outlook changes towards life after he meets Sofia. The daughter of an old friend who leaves Sofia in Count's custody only to never return. Sofia grows old to be a young charming lady. Sofia becomes the Count's biggest purpose. For not only he's proud of her, but at moments could not help but astonish on her talents and intellect. Sofia is going to play Piano in Paris, representing Russian. And Count plans an escape from Metropol.
There are so many elements in this book that I find hard to put down in a short review. This book lets you peep inside Russia in the times of its greatest turmoils. This book lets you sway in Amor Towles amazing description of scenes and playful writing style. I recommend this book to everyone who wants to know what it is to be a Gentleman?
