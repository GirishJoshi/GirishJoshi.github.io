This book reminded me of one of my all time favourite movie It's a Wonderful Life. George Bailey can't take the problems the life is pestering him with anymore, and he decides to end it all by jumping off the bridge. But it's Christmas! And he's rescued by a guardian angel from heaven. You can save someone's life, but how do you save someone who wishes he had never been born? The angel shows him what his town would have looked like if he hadn't been for all the good deeds over the year. And George Bailey learns that the lives of the people in his town and his family would have been vastly different if it not had been for him. The entire town in the end chips in for the loan that Bailey was finding impossible to pay. Everyone in their own capacities tries their best to help him out, why? Because when they all needed him, he was there to help, he was there because he had this life. Bailey's brother makes a toast, "George is the richest man in the town." And indeed he is, for he has friends. The angel leaves Bailey with the message: "no man is a failure who has friends."
I'll be lying if I say I didn't shed tears at the end of the film. And I guess that's just what life is, with the fibre of your being touching mine, and we all shaping each other's realities.
