Review
P.G. Wodehouse. I knew from a long time that this gentleman wrote something that was funny, but I only decided to finally give it a read after an India comic, Rohan Joshi, in a video on his YouTube Channel said, read P.G. Wodehouse if you want to read some of the best slapstick comedy there is.
What ho! This book was humorous, and full of misunderstandings. It was like reading the script of a comedy show. Bertie Wooster who is an aristocrat from London with money enough to never have the need to think about it twice, lives in New York with his personal gentleman Jeeves, who seem to have solution to every trouble Bertie gets into, however, the solutions Jeeves come up with often end up only creating more problems for Bertie. The stories follow a certain formula which is quite predictable, however Wodehouse prose are so effortlessly poetic that you just want to keep reading it, and you don't want to just read it, you want to read it out loud, with the aristocratic accent.
But not all stories in this book are about Bertie and Jeeves, some of them are about Reggie Peppers, however Reggie seems to lack the charm that Bertie possess, and when the book is called My Man Jeeves, you don't really want to read a story that doesn't have Jeeves in it.
There is one thing about comedy genre, come what may, your characters never get hurt. Well, I had this complaint with Hitchhiker's Guide to Galaxy, and I have that same complaint with this book. And that is, you don't really get to connect with the characters because nothing that is happening to them is felt emotionally. But then again, these books are written with the intent to make you laugh and to brighten up your dark days. DON'T TAKE THINGS SO SERIOUSLY. These books are like a good comic or a good meme. THERE IS NO WHY.
And my second complaint with this book is the complaint I often have with classics, you find it hard to relate. With that said, it was a GOODREAD.
