Review
Alain de Bottom takes two archetypical characters and weaves an enduring love story but instead of ending the story with a happily ever after (or the tragic death of one of the lovers), he pulls the curtains from the not so shiny parts of the relationship which romantics never bother to show. He is trying to tell you that everything that you have learned about love from movies, novels, music, and poetry is absurd. The idea of romanticism is absurd and rather fatal to our chances of long-term happiness. He says what we typically call love is only the start of love. And that love is a skill, not just enthusiasm. He goes on to the extent of saying, in many ways marriage is a pretty nasty thing to do to someone you claim to love. He takes you through love as a semester-long university course and as a fine professor, he leaves in awe of your newfound vision to see the world. I recommend this book to anyone looking for the answer to the question: what is love? In my humble opinion, the second most difficult question humans face every day after: why are we here?
