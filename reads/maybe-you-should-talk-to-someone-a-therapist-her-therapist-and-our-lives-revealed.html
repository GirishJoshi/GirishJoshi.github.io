Maybe You Should Talk to Somone leaves me with a warm heart, gently tickling the human in my being. They say there's something likable in everyone. It's impossible to get to know people deeply and not come to like them. That's the essence of this beautifully written book. What is this book about, Girish? A friend had asked me when I shared a quote from this book on my Instagram story. I said I'm still figuring it out, I've figured it out tonight. You might think that this book is about a therapist (by the way therapist and the rapist are spelled the same) seeing a therapist after experiencing a devastating breakup, hoping to bring together her broken pieces, while helping her patients bring together their broken pieces. In essence, you'd be right. But this book is more than a memoir of a therapist. This book is like going to an ophthalmologist and trying different lenses to figure out how to see life in a more palatable way. It's filled with insights, that make you gasp, and contemplate, and rethink your own actions. It's filled with stories, that make you want to not only read them again but to read them to as many people as you can. So you take out your smartphone and record the entire chapter in your voice and send it to your best friends to listen. It's the kind of book when you finish it, you tell yourself, I'll have to read it again. There is just so much I want to experience again, listen again, tell myself again. It rarely happens, this urge of rereading a book. That's what this book is about, knowing that we are here for each other to get through this, whatever it is - life.
Although this book was nonfiction, it felt so real. Surprised I'm calling nonfiction real? We all can believe whatever helps us sleep better. This is one of the best books I read this year, maybe in all the years. I think more words will only diminish what I'm feeling. I just wish I'd read this book a little before. I can't wait to talk to someone about this book.
