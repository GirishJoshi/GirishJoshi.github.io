As we grow old and old, our worlds grow smaller and smaller. With every year passing by the friends and family, you can count on gets fewer in the count. And in the end, you are left all alone, just like you were born. Just like Hubert Bird. And when the last ray of hope is taken away from you, when the last sunshine dies, there is no heart left inside an old man to break. So he packs himself in the four walls of the place he once called home and pretends to live on. I couldn't hold myself from crying as I reached the end of this book. And as I sit to write this review at the midnight I feel a little unsettled. I just cannot stop thinking about what I have just read. I'm not going to tell you much about the story, but I'm going to tell you that it is going to stay with me for a long long time. And whenever a writer is able to do that...leaving the story in the hearts of his/her readers for a long long time, you know, that he/she is one brilliantly fine writer. Thank you Mike Gayle for this book, all the books you have written. I haven't read them, but I am going to. Thank you for breaking my heart and making me cry by telling me the story of this eighty-four-year-old gentleman, Hubert Bird. Thank you for making both of us realize that we are not alone in being alone.
